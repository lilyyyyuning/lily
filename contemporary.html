Contemporary

Boundaries shattered.
From atonality and minimalism to electronic fusion,
this era embraces radical experimentation, diverse voices, and global influences.
Tradition and innovation collide.

Igor Stravinsky

(1882–1971)


A Russian chameleon who fled revolution to become a global citizen. His style evolved from folk-inspired primitivism (The Rite) to neoclassicism (Apollo) and serialism.

Famous works: The Rite of Spring, The Firebird, Symphony of Psalms.
Unique features: Rhythmic violence; jagged harmonies; constantly reinventing tradition. The Rite caused riots in 1913 Paris.

Philip Glass

(b. 1937)


From Baltimore to global minimalism icon, Glass drove taxis while premiering early works. Deeply influenced by Indian raga and Beckett’s theater.

Famous works: Einstein on the Beach, Koyaanisqatsi, Glassworks.
**Unique features": Pulsating repetitive structures; shifting arpeggios; "trance" aesthetics. Brought minimalism from avant-garde to mainstream.

György Ligeti

(1923–2006)


A Hungarian Holocaust survivor who fled communism to Western Europe. His music explores density ("sound masses") and surrealism, famously used in 2001: A Space Odyssey.

Famous works: Atmosphères, Lux Aeterna, Piano Études.
Unique features: "Micropolyphony"—clouds of clustered notes; complex rhythms; cosmic textures blending ancient and futuristic sounds.
