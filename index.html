Baroque

(1600 - 1750)

Characterized by elaborate ornamentation, dramatic expression, and the development of counterpoint.

Classical

(1750 - 1820)

Emphasis on clarity, balance, and form, with melodies that are elegant and well-structured.

Romantic

(1800 - 1850)

Focus on emotional expression, individualism, and the connection to nature and the supernatural.

Impressionism

(1870 - 1886)

Evocative and atmospheric music that suggests moods and impressions rather than concrete narratives.

Contemporary

(1945 - now)

Diverse range of styles and techniques, often experimenting with new sounds and forms.
